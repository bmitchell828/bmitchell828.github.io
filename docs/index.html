About Me

I'm a scientist specializing in inflammatory disease research and pre-clinical model development. Currently, I work as a Scientist at Boehringer Ingelheim, where I develop ex vivo skin and eye models and design assays to study immune activation and local tolerability for drug development.


I earned my PhD from Cornell University, where I focused on inflammatory bowel disease research using pre-clinical mouse models. My work has resulted in multiple publications and grants, including NIH funding. I'm passionate about translating basic research into therapeutic solutions and thrive in collaborative, cross-functional environments. I bring expertise in pre-clinical disease modeling, inflammatory diseases, and integrating Omics methods to solve biological problems.


Outside of work, I love cooking and am a serial hobbyist. Ask me about my current interest, it is probably different than it was a month ago
